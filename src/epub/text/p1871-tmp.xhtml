Truth

v#Come speak to me of Jesus, v##I love that precious name, v#Who built a throne of power v##Upon a cross of shame.

v#Unveil to me the beauty v##That glorifies his face- v#The fullness of the Father v##The image of his grace.

v#My soul would run to meet Him; v##Restrain me not with creeds; v#For Christ, the hope of glory, v##Is what my spirit needs.

v#I need the grand attraction, v##That centres 'round the ciosa, v#To change the gilded things of earth, v##To emptiness and dross.

v#My feet are prone to wander, v##My eyes to turn aside, v#And yet I fain would linger, v##With Christ the crucified.

v#I want a faith that's able v##To stand each storm and shock-- v#A faith forever rooted, v##In Christ the living Rock.

Fifteenth Amendment

v#Beneath the burden of our joy v##Tremble, O wires, from East to West! v#Fashion with words your tongues of fire, v##To tell the nation's high behest.

v#Outstrip the winds, and leave behind v##The murmur of the restless waves; v#Nor tarry with your glorious news, v##Amid the ocean's coral caves.

v#Ring out! ring out! your sweetest chimes, v##Ye bells, that call to prayer; v#Let every heart with gladness thrill, v##And songs of joyful triumph raise.

v#Shake off the dust, rising race! v##Crowned as a brother and a man; v#Justice to-day asserts her claim, v##And from thy brow fades out the ban.

v#With freedom's chrism upon thy head, v##Her precious ensign in thy hand, v#Go place thy once despised name v##Amid the noblest of the land.

v#O ransomed race! give God the praise, v##Who led thee through a crimson sea, v#And ’mid the storm of fire and blood, v##Turned out the war-cloud's light to thee.

Retribution

v#Judgment slumbered. God in mercy v##Stayed his strong avenging hand; v#Sent them priests and sent them prophets, v##But they would not understand.

v#Judgment lingered; men, grown bolder, v##Gloried in their shame and guilt; v#And the blood of God's poor childrep v##Was as water freely spilt.

v#Then arose a cry to heaven, v##Deep and startling, sad and wild, v#Sadder than the wail of Egypt, v##Mourning for the first-born child.

v#For the sighing of the needy v##God at length did bare his hand, v#And the footsteps of his judgments v##Echoed through the guilty land.

v#Oh! the terror, grief and anguish; v##Oh! the bitter, fearful strife, v#When the judgments of Jehovah v##Pressed upon the nation's life.

v#And the land did reel and tremble v##Neath the terror of his frown, v#For its guilt lay heavy on it, v##Pressing like an iron crown.

v#As a warning to the nations, v##Bathed in blood and swathed in tire, v#Lay the once oppressing nation, v##Smitten by God's fearful ire.

The Sin of Achan

v#Night closed o'er the battling army, v##But it brought them no success; v#Victory perched not on their banners; v##Night was full of weariness.

v#Flushed and hopeful in the morning, v##Turned they from their leader's side: v#Routed, smitten and defeated, v##Care they back at eventide.

v#Then in words of bitter mourning v##Joshua's voice soon arose : v#" Tell us, I thou God of Jacob, v##Why this triumph of our foes?”

v#To his pleading came the answer v##Why the hosts in fear did yield: v#" 'Twas because a fearful trespass v##'Mid their tents did lie concealed.”

v#Clear and plain before His vision, v##With whom darkness is as light, v#Lay the spoils that guilty Achan v##Covered from his brethren's sight.

v#From their tents they purged the evil v##That had ruin round them spread; v#Then they won the field of battle, v##Whence they had in terror fled.

v#Through the track of many ages v##Comes this tale of woe and crime; v#Let us read it as a lesson v##And a warning for our time.

v#Oh, for some strong-hearted Joshua ! v##Faithful to his day and time, v#Who will wholly rid the nation v##Of her clinging curse and crime.

v#Till she writes on every banner v##All beneath these folds are free, v#And the oppressed and groaning millions v##Shout the nation's Jubilee.

Lines to Miles O'Reiley

v#You've heard no doubt of Irish bulls, v##And how they blunder, thick and fast; v#But of all the queer and foolish things, v##O'Reiley, you have said the last.

v#You say we brought the rebs supplies, v##And gave them aid amid the fight, v#And if you must be ruled by rebs, v##Instead of black you want them white.

v#You blame us that we did not rise, v##And pluck war from a fiery brand, v#When Little Mac said if we did, v##He'd put us down with iron hand.

v#And when we sought to join your ranks, v##And battle with you, side by side, v#Did men not curl their lips with scorn, v##And thrust us back with hateful pride?

v#And when at last we gained the field, v##Did we not firmly, bravely stand, v#And help to turn the tide of death, v##That spread its ruin o'er the land?

v#We hardly think we're worse than those v##Who kindled up this fearful strife, v#Because we did not seize the chance v##To murder helpless babes and wife,

v#And had we struck, with vengeful hand, v##The rebel where he most could feel, v#Were you not ready to impale v##Our hearts upon your Northern steel?

v#O'Reiley, men like you should wear v##The gift of song like some bright crown, v#Nor worse than ruffians of the ring, v##Strike at a man because he's down.

The Little Builders

v#Ye are builders little builders, v##Not with mortar, brick and stone, v#But your work is far more glorious—- v##Ye are building freedom's throne.

v#Where the ocean never slumbers v##Works the coral 'neath the spray, v#By and by a reef or island v##Rears its head to greet the day

v#Then the balmy rains and sunshine v##Scatter treasures o’er the soil, v#'Till a place for human footprints, v##Crown the little builder's toil.

v#When the stately ships sweep o'er them, v##Cresting all the sea with foam, v#Little think these patient toilers, v##They are building man a home.

v#Do you ask me, precious children, v##How your little hands can build, v#That you love the name of freedom, v##But your fingers are unskilled?

v#Not on thrones or in proud temples, v##Does fair freedom seek her rest; v#No, her chosen habitations, v##Are the hearts that love her best.

v#Would you gain the highest freedom? v##Live for God and man alone, v#Then each heart in freedom's temple, v##Will be like a living stone.

v#Fill your minds with useful knowledge, v##Learn to love the true and right; v#Thus you'll build the throne of freedom, v##On a pedestal of light.

The Dying Child to Her Blind Father

v#Dear father, I hear a whisper, v##It tells me that I must go, v#And my heart returns her answer v##In throbbings so faint and low.

v#I'm sorry to leave you, father, v##I know you will miss me so, v#And the world for you will gather v##A gloomier shade of woe.

v#You will miss me, dearest father, v##When the violets wake from sleep, v#And timidly from their hedges v##The early snow-drops peep,

v#I shall not be here to gather v##The flowers by stream and dell, v#The bright and beautiful flowers, v##Dear Father, you love so well.

v#You will miss my voice, dear father, v##From every earthly tone, v#All the songs that cheered your darkness, v##And you'll be so sad and lone.

v#I can scarcely rejoice, dear father, v##In hope of the brighter land, v#When I know you 'll pine in sadness, v##And miss my guiding hand.

v#You are weeping, dearest father, v##Your sobs are shaking my soul, v#but we'll meet again where the shadow v##And night from your eyes shall roll.

v#And then you will see me, father, v##With visions undimmed and clear, v#Your eyes will sparkle with rapture-- v##You know there's no blindness there.

Light in Darkness

v#Through rifts and rents in our fortune v##We gazed with blinding tears, v#Till glimpses of light and beauty v##Gilded our gloomy fears.

v#An angel stood at our threshold, v##We thought him a child of night, v#Till we saw the print of his steps v##Made lines of living light.

v#We had much the world calls precious; v##We had heaps of shining dust; v#He laid his hand on our treasures, v##And wrote on them moth and rust.
