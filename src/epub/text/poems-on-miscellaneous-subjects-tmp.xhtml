Rizpah, the Daughter of Ai

v#Tidings ! sad tidings for the daughter of Ai, v#They are bearing her prince and loved away, v#Destruction falls like a mournful pall v#On the fallen house of ill-fated Saul.

v#And Rizpah hears that her loved must die, v#But she hears it all with a tearless eye ; v#And clasping her hand with grief and dread v#She meekly bows her queenly head.

v#The blood has left her blanching cheek, v#Her quivering lips refuse to speak, v#Oh ! grief like hers has learned no tone— v#A world of grief is all its own.

v#But the deed is done, and the hand is stay'd v#That havoc among the brethren made. v#And Rizpah takes her lowly seat v#To watch the princely dead at her feet.

v#The jackall crept out with a stealthy tread, v#To batten and feast on the noble dead ; v#The vulture bore down with a heavy wing v#To dip his beak in life's stagnant spring.

v#The hyena heard the jackall's howl, v#And he bounded forth with a sullen growl, v#When Rizpah's shriek rose on the air v#Like a tone from the caverns of despair.

v#She sprang from her sad and lowly seat, v#For a moment her heart forgot to beat. v#And the blood rushed up to her marble cheek . v#And a flash to her eye so sad and meek.

v#The vulture paused in his downward flight, v#As she raised her form to its queenly height. v#The hyena's eye had a horrid glare v#As he turned again to his desert lair.

v#The jackall slunk back with a quickened tread, v#From his cowardly search of Rizpah's dead; v#Unsated he turned from the noble prey, v#Subdued by a glance of the daughter of Ai.
