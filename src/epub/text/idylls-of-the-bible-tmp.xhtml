Deliverance

v#Rise up! rise up! Oh Israel, v##Let a spotless lamb be slain; v#The angel of death will o'er you bend v##And rend your galling chain.

v#Sprinkle its blood upon the posts v##And lintels of your door ; v#When the angel sees the crimson spots v##Unharmed he will pass you o'er.

v#Gather your flocks and herds to-night, v##Your children by  our side : v#A leader from Arabia comes v##To be your friend and guide.

v#With girded loins and sandlcd feet v##Await the hour of dread, v#When Mizraim shall wildly mourn v##Her first-born and her dead.

v#The sons of Abraham no more v##Shall crouch 'neath Pliaroah's hand, v#Trembling with agony and dread, v##He'll thrust you from the land.

v#And ye shall hold in unborn years v##A feast to mark this day, v#When joyfully the fathers rose v##And cast their chains away.

v#When crimson tints of morning flush v##The golden gates of day, v#Or gorgeous hue of even melt v##In sombre shades away,

v#Then ye shall to your children teach v##The meaning of this feast. v#How from the proud oppressor's hand v##Their fathers were released,

v#And ye shall hold through distant years v##This feast with glad accord, v#And children's children yet shall learn v##To love and trust the Lord.

v#Ages have passed since Israel trod v##In triumph through the sea, v#And yet they hold in memory's urn v##Their first great jubilee.

v#When Moses led the ransomed hosts, v##And Miriam's song arose. v#While ruin closed around the path v##Of their pursuing foes.

v#Shall Israel thro' long varied years v##These memories cherish yet, v#And we who lately stood redeemed v##Our broken chains forget?

v#Should we forget the wondrous change v##That to our people came, v#When justice rose and sternly plead v##Our cause with sword and flame ?

v#And led us through the storms of war v##To freedom's fairer shore, v#When slavery sank beneath a flood v##Whose waves were human gore.

v#Oh, youth and maidens of the land, v##Rise up with one accord, v#And in the name of Christ go forth v##To battle for the Lord.

v#Go forth, but not in crimson fields. v##With fratricidal strife, v#But in the name of Christ go forth v##For freedom, love and life.

v#Go forth to follow in his steps, v##Who came not to destroy, v#Till wastes shall blossom as the rose, v##And deserts sing for joy.

Simon's Feast

v#He is coming, she said, to Simon's feast. v##The prophet of Galilee, v#Though multitudes around him throng v##In longing his face to see.

v#He enters the home as Simon's guest, v##But he gives no welcome kiss ; v#He brings no water to bathe his feet — v##Why is Simon so remiss ?

v#The prophet's face is bright with love, v##And mercy beams from his eye ; v#He pities the poor, the lame and blind, v##An outcast, I will draw nigh.

v#If a prophet, he will surely know v##The guilt of my darkened years ; v#With broken heart I'll seek his face, v##And bathe his feet with my tears.

v#No holy rabbi lays his hand v##In blessing on my head ; v#No loving voice floats o'er the path, v##The downward path I tread.

v#Unto the Master's side she pressed, v##A penitent, frail and fair, v#Rained on his feet a flood of tears. v##And then wiped them with her hair.

v#Over the face of Simon swept v##An air of puzzled surprise ; v#Can my guest a holy prophet be, v##And not this woman despise?

v#Christ saw the thoughts that Simon's heart v##Had written upon his face, v#Kindly turned to the sinful one v##In her sorrow and disgrace.

v#Where Simon only saw the stains, v##Where sin and shame were rife, v#Christ looked beneath and saw the germs v##Of a fair, outflowering life.

v#Like one who breaks a galling chain, v##And sets a prisoner free, v#He rent her fetters with the words, v##''Thy sins are forgiven thee."

v#God be praised for the gracious words v##Which came through that woman's touch v#That souls redeemed thro' God's dear Son v##May learn to love him so much ;

v#That souls once red with guilt and crime v##May their crimson stains outgrow ; v#The scarlet spots upon their lives v##Become whiter than driven snow.
